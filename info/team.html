Meet the advisors and operating team for Boston Startup School.
Katie is Managing Director of TechStars in Boston and also a founder of Project 11, a firm that invests in and assists early-stage startups. She has spent her career building significant Internet businesses in the community, self-publishing, and search spaces. Katie pioneered early freemium business models at Lycos for the Tripod and Angelfire communities. She learned the ropes of product and business development at AltaVista, RagingBull, Zip2 and Mirror Worlds. Katie holds an MBA from Yale University and a BA in Biology from Oberlin College.
Reed was recently founding Director of Microsoft Startup Labs. Before that, he was Chief Technology Officer of EONS, Inc., Managing Director and Vice President of Technology for Idealab, which founded several companies including Picasa and Compete, and co-founder of Radnet and RadioAMP. He began his career as architect and designer of Freelance Graphics, the best selling presentation package acquired by Lotus Development. Reed is an MIT dropout and holds one patent that should probably never have been granted.
Mark teaches a wide range of topics, from integrated circuit design to mobile application development, at Olin College. He resides at the intersection of design, engineering, and entrepreneurship. Mark advises startups and is prototyping the future of news at The Globe Lab. He has consulted at MITRE, Roundware, and AppTek. Mark has a PhD in Electrical Engineering at the University of Washington, a MS in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Northwestern and a BS in the same at John Hopkins.
Aaron is Head of Special Projects at TechStars in Boston. He most recently helped build Le Camping, the first startup accelerator in France headquartered in the former Paris Stock Exchange. Aaron was an Associate at Palm Ventures, a key member at LivingSocial, and a founding member of BuyYourFriendADrink.com, an online to offline consumer marketing company used by leading beverage brands across the country. He has a BA in Business Management with a focus on Innovation and Entrepreneurship from Clark University.
Shaun has encouraged entrepreneurial thinking and activity, conveyed valuable lessons learned from current and past entrepreneurial people and organizations, and established new ventures as an Entrepreneur-in-Residence at Georgetown University. He was an Associate at TechStars in Boston and a Senior Consultant at Booz Allen Hamilton, where he delivered effective strategies for the optimization and securing of technology to clients in various spaces. Shaun has a MS in Information Systems and Leadership Development at John Hopkins and a BS in both Computer Science and Sociology at Georgetown University.
Will founded the landscaping business, All Pro Services. He was a tenth grade Math Teacher for Teach for America at the Denver School of Science and Technology. At Better Lessons, Inc. he conducted market research in the education software industry to aid with product expansion and redesign. Will is an MBA student at Babson College and has a BBA in Accounting at The College of William and Mary.
John started at GroupGain, a social network-oriented e-commerce startup. He assisted in the development and maintenance of the company's social media presence and has experience in business development and marketing. John has managed the social media presence of the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) at Babson College, which helped establish GEM as a modern-day, international, interactive portal for entrepreneurial research. He was an Associate at TechStars in Boston and is studying business at Babson College.
Dee also acts as Office Manager at TechStars in Boston. She has fifteen years experience in sales, project management, and market research in Germany, the United States and South East Asia. As a teaching assistant at MIT, Dee contributed towards the course development and learning experience of students in the Global Classroom on Sustainability and Consumer Behavior. She volunteered at Boston City Hall, helped launch the Boston Green Awards in 2007, and co-authored a clean mobility and transportation report. Dee co-founded MobyFab with her husband and has become a published author in the Amazon KDP program in Germany. She earned an MS in Biology at Martin Luther University of Halle-Wittenberg and an MBA at the MIT Sloan School of Management.
